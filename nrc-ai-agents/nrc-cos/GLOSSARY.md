# NRC-COS Glossary

Canonical definitions for terms used across NRC-COS. Each entry reflects how the term is actually used in the frozen documents — this glossary does not redefine anything, it indexes what's already established.

**Accessibility** — the Principles-layer document extending every other Principle's standard to hold true regardless of who a person is or how they encounter NRC's work; not a separate obligation, but a refusal to let the existing standard degrade based on the audience.

**ADR (Decision Record)** — a lightweight, permanent, append-only record of one architecturally significant decision: context, decision, alternatives considered, trade-offs, expected impact, and related documents. Never edited after acceptance, only superseded.

**Archive** — the layer holding what NRC-COS used to believe or build. Nothing is deleted from this system; superseded content moves here with a record of what replaced it and why.

**Concept Exploration (Framework)** — the Framework-layer process for generating several genuinely distinct creative directions, evaluating each on its own terms, and selecting one with the rejected alternatives' reasoning preserved, not erased.

**Constitution (Design Constitution)** — the Phase 0 document translating Brand Philosophy into enforceable Constitutional Laws and Prohibitions; the standard every creative decision must satisfy, regardless of medium.

**Creative Debt** — the accumulated gap between what NRC-COS documents and what is actually true in practice; reviewed on a standing cadence, resolved by fixing the work, formally excepting it, or revising the principle that no longer holds.

**Cross-Cutting Principle** — a Principles-layer document (Accessibility, Performance Standards) that applies every other Principle universally along one dimension — who is encountering the work, or how much time it costs them — rather than owning a domain of its own the way Color or Typography does.

**Decisions** — the layer holding every ADR; the permanent, chronological record of why NRC-COS is shaped the way it is.

**Design Language** — the Phase 0 document describing the recognizable creative character produced by consistently following the Design Constitution: what NRC's work should feel like, not what it must do.

**Discovery (Framework)** — the Framework-layer process for laddering from a surface want down to the deepest, most universal transformation a piece of work actually creates.

**Domain (Principle)** — a single area of enduring rule-making within the Principles layer — motion, color, typography, and so on — each owned by exactly one document.

**Framework** — a reusable structure for organizing and sequencing Principles to solve a recurring category of problem, independent of any single Product.

**Freeze** — the point at which a document or phase is declared complete and immutable except through the ADR process, following an architectural review and any required revisions.

**Hierarchy** — the Visual Language principle that one thing should carry the most weight at any given moment, with everything else visibly, deliberately subordinate; extended across the layer into Motion ("hierarchy across time") and Performance ("hierarchy, expressed in time instead of size").

**Invitation (Framework)** — the Framework-layer structure for the moment NRC asks for commitment, sized to the weight of what's actually being asked.

**Justification (Law)** — the constitutional requirement that every creative decision have a statable reason for existing, distinct from "it looks good" or "it's expected."

**Narrative (Framework)** — the Framework-layer structure for sequencing an experience over time: acts, emotional peaks and troughs, transition types, and how a motion budget is spent unevenly across a sequence.

**North Star** — the single-page, maximally compressed distillation of the entire Philosophy layer; the one document that should be enough to rebuild NRC's judgment if every other document disappeared.

**Pattern** — a proven, reusable recipe extracted from a Product, written only after the same specific solution has succeeded independently in a second, genuinely different Product. Never written speculatively.

**Philosophy** — the layer establishing why NRC exists and what must remain true regardless of medium, technology, or scale; the constitutional foundation everything else derives from.

**Principle** — an enduring, medium-agnostic rule translating Philosophy into a testable standard for one domain of the work.

**Product** — a specific, real thing built by applying Frameworks and Principles to actual content, audience, and constraints; a one-off application, not a reusable structure.

**Promotion** — the governance mechanism by which an idea advances through the Creative Lifecycle — Concept, Experiment, Implementation, Review, Decision, Pattern, Principle — gated at each stage, never automatic.

**Proof (Framework)** — the Framework-layer structure for presenting evidence of past work so it earns trust rather than performs it.

**Representation** — the Principles-layer domain governing the truthfulness of images — photography, illustration, renders, generated imagery, diagrams — a claim about what's real that must be honest or corrected.

**Restraint (Law)** — the constitutional requirement that addition is never the default response to a problem; includes proportion, meaning scale must match what's being communicated, never what available tools make possible.

**Retirement** — the discipline governing how principles, patterns, and documents are removed or superseded: nothing deleted, everything moved to Archive with a recorded reason.

**Substance (Law)** — the constitutional requirement that confidence in how something is presented must never outpace confidence in what is actually being presented.

**Voice** — the Principles-layer domain governing how language is created — sentence construction, honesty, restraint — distinct from Typography, which governs how already-written language becomes visible.

**Weight** — visual, typographic, or temporal significance assigned to something; per Visual Language, weight must follow real significance, never whatever is easiest to make prominent.
