# NRC Invitation Framework

## Purpose

Invitation begins only once Discovery, Concept Exploration, Narrative, and Proof have all completed — the truth has been found, its strongest expression selected, its unfolding sequenced, and belief in it earned. This Framework's responsibility is what happens at the end of that chain: making the next action feel like the natural, earned consequence of everything already established, not a demand introduced at the last moment.

Invitation is not persuasion through pressure. It is the disciplined transition from understanding to action — and it can only responsibly exist once understanding has actually been built by everything upstream of it.

## When Invitation Begins

Invitation begins once Narrative has determined where it belongs in the sequence and once Proof has reached its own completion — once, by Proof's own signals, doubt in the claimed transformation is no longer reasonable. This Framework does not independently re-verify that belief has been earned, nor does it reposition itself within the sequence; it trusts Narrative's placement and Proof's completion the same way Proof trusted the position Narrative assigned it, and Narrative trusted the direction Concept Exploration selected.

If belief has not actually been earned by that point, Invitation does not begin. There is nothing yet for it to responsibly build on.

## What Invitation Is Trying to Achieve

The test is whether someone who has genuinely understood everything the experience already established would arrive at the next action largely on their own — not whether the invitation is persuasive enough to talk them into it. An invitation that has to work hard to be convincing is compensating for something insufficient earlier in the experience, and no amount of persuasive skill at this final stage can repair that. The fix belongs upstream, not here.

The output is a next step that feels like a continuation of understanding already reached, sized and worded to match exactly what has actually been earned — nothing manufactured to make it feel more urgent or necessary than it genuinely is.

## Characteristics of Strong Invitations

A strong invitation is sized to what is actually being asked. A small, low-consequence next step should be offered lightly; a significant commitment should be asked for with the weight that commitment actually carries. Treating either as though it were the other misrepresents what's really being requested.

A strong invitation is clear about what happens next. Someone accepting it should already know, before accepting, what they are agreeing to — not discover the real shape of the commitment afterward.

A strong invitation carries no manufactured urgency. Whatever timing pressure exists, if any, must be genuinely true, not introduced because true urgency wasn't available to make the case.

## Readiness

Readiness is a property of the audience at the moment Invitation begins, not something this Framework creates. It is entirely inherited from what Discovery, Concept Exploration, Narrative, and Proof have already built — whether someone has actually been given everything they need to decide, or whether Invitation is being asked to compensate for groundwork that was never completed.

This Framework's responsibility is to recognize readiness honestly, not to manufacture its appearance. If readiness isn't there, the honest response is to say less, not to push harder.

## Commitment

What Invitation asks for should match exactly what the relationship built so far has actually earned — no more, no less. Asking for more commitment than has been earned treats trust as already established that hasn't been; asking for less than the moment actually calls for understates a decision's real weight and can read as evasive about what's really being requested.

The commitment asked for should be the most honest possible description of what happens if someone says yes, not the version most likely to make saying yes easy.

Participation — a smaller, more exploratory next step — is not a separate category from commitment. It is commitment at its lightest weight, governed by the same requirement: sized honestly to what is actually being asked, never inflated into more significance than it carries.

## Signals Invitation Is Complete

Invitation is complete when the audience could state, in their own words, exactly what they are being asked to do and why — an invitation that cannot be restated plainly has not actually landed, however persuasively it was delivered.

It is complete when the invitation does not require urgency or pressure to be compelling on its own — if it does, something is being compensated for that a strong invitation should never need to compensate for.

It is complete when what's being asked matches, without inflation or understatement, what has actually been earned by everything that came before it.

## Failure Modes

**Manufacturing urgency to compensate for insufficient proof or narrative.** Using pressure to accomplish what belief should have already accomplished on its own.

**Asking for more commitment than has been earned.** Requesting a scale of action the relationship built so far doesn't actually support.

**Asking for less than the moment calls for.** Treating a genuinely significant decision as trivial, which understates its real weight and can read as avoidance.

**Leaving the next step unclear.** An invitation someone cannot restate in their own words has failed the same test any communication in this system must pass.

**Proceeding as though proof were unnecessary.** Assuming a well-told narrative is persuasive enough on its own, without the belief Proof is specifically responsible for establishing first.

## Relationship to Philosophy

This Framework applies the Substance Law directly to a final claim: an invitation must never assert more urgency or necessity than genuinely exists.

It applies the Restraint Law to the size of the ask itself — inviting exactly what's needed, never more, mirrors the same discipline this system applies to every other addition.

It applies the Justification Law to the next step specifically — whatever action is being requested needs its own clear reason, traceable to everything already established before it.

## Relationship to Principles

Interaction Principles governs this Framework more directly than any other document in the Principles layer. Its requirement that the weight of commitment match the weight of the consequence, that consequences be knowable before they're chosen, and that interaction never be requested for its own sake collectively describe almost exactly what a strong invitation must do. Motion Language's prohibition on manufacturing urgency the content hasn't earned applies here without need for translation — an invitation is simply the moment that prohibition matters most.

## Relationship to Narrative Framework

Narrative determines where within a sequence an invitation belongs and what should have already happened before it arrives — the same kind of structural handoff Narrative gives to Proof. Invitation receives that placement as given: it does not reposition itself within the sequence, and it does not act sooner than the sequence has actually prepared for. If the placement Narrative assigned doesn't leave enough already established for an invitation to feel earned once attempted, that tension belongs back with Narrative, not resolved here by asking anyway.

## Relationship to Proof Framework

Invitation receives established belief from Proof as a given input — it does not re-argue or re-verify the credibility Proof already built. If asking for commitment doesn't feel earned once attempted, that is diagnostic information pointing back to Proof, or further upstream still, never something Invitation should paper over with more persuasive pressure at the point of asking.

## Relationship to Products

Invitation determines the structural shape and discipline of an ask — its size, its clarity, its honesty about what happens next. It does not determine the literal form that ask takes in any specific medium: the exact wording, the specific mechanism someone uses to say yes, or how that mechanism looks or behaves. Those decisions belong to Products, which apply this Framework's discipline to something real, the same way every other Framework in this system hands off execution rather than performing it.
