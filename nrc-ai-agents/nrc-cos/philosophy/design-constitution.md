# NRC Design Constitution

## Purpose

Brand Philosophy explains why NRC exists. This document exists to make that philosophy enforceable.

A belief is not a standard. This document turns belief into law — a set of tests every creative decision must pass, regardless of medium, client, deadline, or what technology currently makes possible. These laws do not change because a project is difficult, a timeline is short, or a trend is current. If a project cannot satisfy one, the project changes, or the exception is documented through the decision-record process. The law itself does not bend quietly.

## Constitutional Laws

**The Justification Law.** Every creative decision must have a statable reason for existing. If the only available answer is that it looks good, that it is expected, or that it is available, it has not met the standard — regardless of how well it is executed.

**The Restraint Law.** Addition is never the default response to a problem. Before anything is added, it must be established that nothing already present, and nothing that could be removed instead, would solve the problem on its own. This applies to scale as much as to presence: the intensity, complexity, or spectacle of a decision must be proportionate to what it is communicating — never to what current tools happen to make possible. A capability being available is not a reason to use it.

**The Substance Law.** No creative decision may claim more than the underlying business can support. Confidence in how something is presented must never outpace confidence in what is being presented.

**The Legibility Law.** Every creative decision must be traceable to a person accountable for it. Work with no identifiable author has not met the standard, no matter how strong it appears.

**The Timelessness Law.** A creative decision is judged against whether it would still be defensible in ten years — not against how well it performs against what is currently fashionable. Relevance to a trend is never sufficient justification on its own.

## Creative Obligations

What must always be true of how NRC's work gets made:

- State the reason for a decision before it is approved, not after it is questioned.
- Test every addition against whether removing something else would achieve the same result first.
- Verify a claim against what the business can actually support before it is asserted.
- Keep the author of every significant decision identifiable, never diffused into "the team."
- Evaluate durability before shipping — ask whether a decision holds up independent of what is currently popular.
- Match the weight of a decision to the weight of what it communicates, not to what the available tools make easy.
- Check new work against what NRC has already established. Surface contradictions. Do not let them stand unaddressed.

## Creative Prohibitions

Constitutional Laws can be tested, and — through the decision-record process — deliberately and visibly excepted for a documented reason. The following cannot. These are not standards to be weighed against circumstance. They are boundaries. Crossing one is not an exception. It is a violation.

- Never present a claim about a business that is not supported by what it actually delivers.
- Never let a creative decision exist with no identifiable person accountable for it.
- Never adopt another studio's or brand's identifiable creative signature as a substitute for arriving at NRC's own judgment.
- Never accept "this is what everyone else does" as justification on its own.
- Never let cost or speed alone stand as the reason a creative decision was made. They may shape how something is achieved. They are never why it was chosen.

## Decision Tests

Before approving any creative decision, at any scale:

- Could I explain, out loud, why this exists?
- If I removed this, would the business's substance become less clear — or would nothing change?
- Does this claim more than the business can actually support?
- Can I name the person accountable for this choice?
- Would this still be defensible in ten years, independent of what is currently popular?
- Is the scale of this decision proportionate to what it is actually communicating?

If any answer is uncertain, the decision is not ready.

## Relationship to Brand Philosophy

Brand Philosophy establishes why NRC exists and what it believes. This document does not restate those beliefs — it exists to make them testable. Where Brand Philosophy asks whether a decision is true to NRC, this document asks whether a decision can be defended, examined, and held to a standard. A decision that satisfies this constitution should, by construction, already be consistent with the philosophy it derives from. A decision that violates this constitution is never acceptable regardless of how well it appears to serve the philosophy in isolation.

## Relationship to the Remainder of NRC-COS

Every document that follows — Design Language, the Principles layer, Frameworks, Patterns, and Experiences — must satisfy every law and prohibition established here. This document is not cited for *why* NRC does something; Brand Philosophy answers that. It is cited for whether a specific decision, in any domain, is acceptable at all. Nothing downstream may loosen a law or a prohibition established here. Downstream documents may only make these laws more specific to a domain — never less binding.
