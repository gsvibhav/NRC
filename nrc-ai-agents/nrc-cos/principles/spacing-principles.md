# NRC Spacing Principles

## Purpose

Visual Language establishes that space is used deliberately, not left over. This document goes further: it explains how the space between things — not just the space around any one thing — carries its own meaning. Two things placed close together are read as related before anyone consciously decides they are. This document explains the enduring rules behind that reading, independent of grid, platform, or medium.

## Proximity Implies Relationship

Things placed close together are understood as belonging to each other before any label or explanation confirms it. This happens before conscious reading — it is the first structure a person perceives, ahead of the words or images inside it. Because of this, proximity is not a neutral choice. Placing two things near each other is already a claim that they relate; making that claim without meaning it is a kind of dishonesty the reader will believe before they have a chance to question it.

## Separation Must Be Deliberate, Not Incidental

When two things are meant to be read as distinct, the space between them must be unambiguous about that — different enough from the space used to signal closeness elsewhere that it cannot be mistaken for it. A gap that is almost, but not quite, the same as a gap used to signal relationship elsewhere creates a false reading the person will trust without noticing they've been misled.

## Density Must Be Justified Like Any Other Decision

Compressing things together should only happen when the compression itself is true — when the things really do belong that closely to one another. Density used simply to fit more into less space describes a relationship that doesn't actually exist. Space, like anything else NRC decides to include or withhold, needs a reason; crowding without one is not efficiency, it is a false claim about how closely related things are.

## Openness Signals Structural Confidence

Where Visual Language establishes that the space given to any one thing signals how much it matters, this extends to the composition as a whole: a structure with room to breathe is a structure that trusts its own organization to be understood without being packed for reassurance. Density applied evenly, everywhere, is what a composition looks like when it doesn't trust its own hierarchy to hold up on its own.

## Rhythm Comes From Consistent Relationships

The same kind of relationship should be given the same kind of space, every time it occurs — and a different kind of relationship should read as visibly different. Rhythm in a spatial composition is not a matter of every gap being identical; it is the pattern that emerges when consistent relationships produce consistent space, and the variation between different relationships stays legible rather than arbitrary.

## Absence Communicates as Directly as Presence

Space with nothing in it is not a gap waiting to be filled — it is frequently the decision itself, and the strongest available statement that nothing further needs to be added. An empty area is not evidence that something was forgotten. Left deliberately, it says as much as anything that could have been placed there instead.

## Relationship to Visual Language and Typography Principles

Visual Language establishes that the space around a single thing signals its importance. Typography Principles establishes that language deserves room to stand on its own. This document extends both into the space between multiple things — how proximity, separation, and density describe the relationships among them, not just the standing of any one on its own. None of these three documents should be read as governing different territory; each governs the same underlying belief about space, applied at a different scale.

## Relationship to Later Principles

Grid systems, margins, and measured scales belong to future Frameworks and implementation documents, which will translate these relationships into specific, medium-appropriate values. Nothing in those documents should establish a different reason for space to exist than the ones described here — only the specific measurements through which these relationships get expressed in a given medium.
